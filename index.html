Irene Iodice

I am a PostDoc, working for the ANR Project Moquat on Globalisation, Product Quality and Employment Effects (PI: Maria Bas). I recieved my PhD in June 2020 under a joint program between Sant'Anna School of Advanced Studies and Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne. My work has been advised by Lionel Fontagné and Giovanni Dosi.

My research focuses on patterns of international trade from firm heterogenous responses to international competition and regulatory regimes. I have been working on (i) the dynamics of Italian wages within the manufacturing sector, (ii) the effects of not transparent domestic technical regulations on the exporting activity and finally on (iii) the link between firms' performance in international markets and product portfolio strategies.
